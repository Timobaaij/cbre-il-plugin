# Extended Research Question Bank (v7 - business-intelligence brief)

Work the full list systematically. **Fetch the company IR page first.** Do not search reactively. Each question is tagged with the owning agent. The aim is the raw material for a proper company brief that establishes who the company is, what drives it strategically, its financial health and competitive position, AND THEN how its supply chain works, its biggest challenges, how those land on real estate, the current property set-up, stakeholders, and the latest movements. The corporate-profile questions (sections below) carry equal weight to the real-estate ones; do not under-research them.

## Company profile (all company types - feeds Act II)
A. **Strategy and identity.** The stated corporate mission/vision (quote from the latest annual report or investor presentation). The 3 to 5 strategic priorities the CEO or board has communicated for the current period (source earnings calls, annual report, capital-markets day). The overall growth model (organic / M&A-led / franchise or partnership-led / mixed). The major strategic shifts or pivots of the last 2 to 3 years (digital, sustainability, portfolio restructuring, geographic rebalancing). How management talks about the FOCUS REGION specifically within the global strategy (growth engine, mature cash generator, or under review). **(R3, R1)**
B. **Business model and revenue architecture.** What the company sells, to whom, and through which channels (stores, e-commerce, wholesale, direct B2B, distributors). The revenue model (product margin, service fees, licensing, subscription, mixed). The channel mix in the focus region and how it has shifted in 2 to 3 years. The pricing position (value, mid-market, premium) and what it implies for service levels and network design. By type: retailer format; manufacturer/B2B end customers and how customer type shapes logistics. **(R3)**
C. **Corporate structure.** Focus-region HQ location and legal entity name; key subsidiaries and the countries they cover; brands/product lines under the regional umbrella; whether the region is run as one or split into sub-regions (DACH, CEE, Southern Europe, Nordics, etc.); any restructuring of the regional entity in the last 24 months; regional headcount/workforce scale if disclosed. **(R3, R4)**
D. **Financial performance (multi-year table).** The most recent 2 to 3 years for: revenue, revenue growth YoY, EBITDA or operating (or Adjusted EBITA) margin, capex, and net debt / leverage ratio. Group level always; focus-region level if separately reported, else state clearly that it is not and provide group only. Capture the exact figure and the source/as-of date for each. **(R1)**
E. **Revenue by geography.** The top 5 markets in the focus region by revenue (with % of regional revenue where available); which markets are growing fastest (by revenue, store count, or volume as fits the type); and any markets management flags as mature or under rationalisation. **(R1, R3)**

## Core (all company types)
1. Latest annual and quarterly results: revenue, margin, net income, free cash flow, and what they say about physical footprint and capital discipline. **(R1)**
2. Strategy in the company's OWN WORDS: the current plan, its pillars, capex guidance, and the medium-term targets (quote them). Where is it heading? **(R1, R3)**
3. How does the supply chain actually work (the operating model)? What is manufactured in-house and where; what is outsourced; named 3PLs and developers and who holds the lease in each case; service-parts vs finished-goods flows. **(R2)**
4. Facility network: count, typology, locations, sizes, and the European core in detail. **(R2)**
5. Recent movements, last 18-24 months: a dated register of openings, closures, expansions, relocations, restructurings and major announcements, each with its real-estate implication. **(R2, R3)**
6. The biggest challenges and pressures the company faces (cost, regulatory, litigation, tariffs, geopolitics, talent), in its own and analysts' words. **(R3)**
7. Current real estate set-up: owned vs leased split; IFRS 16 lease liability, right-of-use assets, weighted-average term, discount rate, and the maturity schedule; any sale-and-leaseback programme (existence, scope, roll-off); property carrying value. **(R1, R2)**
8. Real estate governance: who runs corporate real estate, supply chain, logistics and facilities; the reporting line into the C-suite; how decisions are made; any incumbent advisor or IFM provider. **(R4)**
9. Has CBRE been involved with this company in any public capacity? Acknowledge incumbents honestly. **(R4)**
10. ESG, net-zero, decarbonisation and circularity commitments that drive facility retrofit, reuse, or new-build specification. **(R3, R2)**
11. Competitive landscape: 3 to 5 named peers; where the company LEADS versus where it is a CHALLENGER (be market-specific); recent competitive moves (competitor expansions, exits, M&A) that change the landscape; the peers' footprint and supply-chain stance; and what the positioning implies for the urgency or nature of supply-chain investment. Cite at least two independent source views for the central thesis. **(R3)**

## By company type (R2, R3)
- **Manufacturers / industrial:** capacity and where new capacity is built; reshoring/nearshoring/regionalisation; raw-material and inbound logistics; sustainability-driven facility change.
- **Retailers / restaurants:** store count and opening rate; stores-per-DC; e-commerce penetration and fulfilment model; format and private-label shifts.
- **B2B distributors / wholesalers:** branch/depot count and revenue-per-depot; SLA changes; contract wins/losses; insourcing.
- **E-commerce / omnichannel:** online penetration trajectory; delivery commitments; fulfilment insourcing; micro-fulfilment / dark stores / last-mile build.

## Competitor triangulation (R3, Synthesis)
Cite at least two independent source views for the central thesis. Do not let the core argument rest on a single source. Feeds the competitive slide and the G8 omission check.

## Language requirement (R5)
For non-English-headquartered companies, run at least three searches in the home-market language, especially for facility announcements and planning applications, which are often only reported in local media. Preserve material quotes in the original language with an English translation in brackets immediately after.
