# Source Priority Hierarchy

When researching, prioritise sources in this order. Higher-ranked sources take precedence when data conflicts. **Record the tier number against every source record.**

1. **Annual / interim reports and investor presentations** — the gold standard for financials, facility counts, and strategic plans. Include earnings call transcripts.
2. **Company press releases and investor relations pages** — start every research session by searching for the company's IR page and fetching it.
3. **Planning applications, public filings, and regulatory disclosures** — lease liabilities, right-of-use assets, IFRS 16 disclosures, national and municipal planning portals.
4. **Developer and 3PL press releases** — confirm committed projects and named developers.
5. **Trade press** — Property Week, React News, Logistics Manager, Supply Chain Dive, and equivalent regional publications.
6. **General business news** — Reuters, Bloomberg, FT, local business press.

**Disregard** SEO-driven listicles, generic company-profile aggregators, and unsourced blog posts. If a claim appears only in a low-quality source, flag it as unverified and do not place it on a slide as fact.

## Trace to the highest tier

The verification gate (see `evidence-standard.md`) requires tracing each claim to the **highest tier on which it should exist**, not to whatever was cited first.

- Financials, facility counts, lease disclosures, stakeholder roles, and announced projects should all exist at tier 1-2. If a claim of this kind rests only on a tier 5-6 source, find the primary source and re-cite to it, or flag the claim as thin.
- A figure that originates in a press release (tier 2 or 4) and is then repeated by trade press (tier 5) is still a tier 2 or 4 claim; cite the originator, not the echo.
- The ledger's tier column records the tier the claim was *verified at*, which feeds the confidence rubric's source-tier test.

## Date discipline

Two distinct dates per source record:

- **Publication / as-of date** — when the source was published or last current. This is what the recency test measures age from. A 2023 annual report's facility count is a 2023-dated claim even if you fetch it today.
- **Retrieved date** — when you fetched it. Used for audit and reproducibility only.

Where a source carries no date, record "undated" and treat it as a recency penalty in the confidence rubric, not as recent.
