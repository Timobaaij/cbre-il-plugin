# The QA Gates (v6 — independent, shift-left, defense in depth)

The governing principle of the review architecture is **separation of author and reviewer**: any check that is a *judgement about work the orchestrator authored* runs in an **isolated sub-agent with fresh context**. Deterministic invariants stay as **scripts** (no judgement to bias). The orchestrator only *adjudicates* verdicts (fix / strike / deviate-with-written-reason) and owns the ledger and assembly. **The orchestrator never self-certifies a judgement review of its own plan.** This is the fix for the old design, where QA1-QA4 ran inline in the orchestrator thread.

The second principle is **shift-left**: catch each defect at the layer where it lives and as early as it can exist. Two review layers run *before any slide is built* (on the narrative outline, then on the content plan), so "this deck says nothing / the read is wrong" is caught for the price of a paragraph, not a rebuild.

Owners: **Script** = deterministic check in `helpers/gate_runner.py` / `final_gate.py`, run in-thread. **Indep** = isolated sub-agent, fresh context, dispatched by the orchestrator. **Orch** = adjudication and assembly only.

| Gate | Owner | Inspects | Checks | Blocks on |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **G0 Narrative-outline review** | Indep (Opus) | `narrative_outline.md` | thesis + through-line; the chapter breakdown and slide allocation fit the story (the right events are told as dedicated prose-led scenes, nothing over- or under-weighted); the answer contract is covered (the executive read, big movers, supply-chain signature and opportunity intersection included) and the three persona slides (CEO / CSCO / Head of Real Estate) are present, substantive and placed at their act capstones; every slide earns its place and passes the I&L "so what"; intelligence is dense and non-obvious; no glaring omission vs the fact base; honest about thin areas | RED |
| **G1a Source integrity** | Script | plan + ledger | back-fills the ledger `slide` column from the plan first (so it is never stale), then: every factual element cites a resolving Claim ID; rows have URL/tier/retrieved date; bands present; no struck-claim citation | any defect |
| **G1b Source-verification judgement** | Indep (Opus) | plan + ledger + fetched sources | trace each claim to the highest tier on which it should exist and confirm the figure appears there; tier 5-6-only = thin; unconfirmable = struck to readiness | unverifiable claim on a slide |

| **G5 Reconciliation** | Script | rendered deck text + plan + ledger | no number/claim on a slide absent from the plan; every `[N]` resolves; no citation drift | new/altered claim |

**Where the script inputs come from.** The *deck text* G5/G6a/G6b inspect is `deck_text.txt`, which `build_deck.py` writes next to the `.pptx` on every build (one line per rendered paragraph/table cell); regenerate it from an existing deck with `build_deck.py --dump-text <pptx> deck_text.txt`. The *forecast re-derivation* G3 runs (only when the module is on) reads `forecast_inputs.json` (the sourced inputs) beside `forecasting_worklog.md`: `gate_runner.py qa3` re-runs `helpers/forecasting.py` on it and blocks on a missing inputs file, an input with no Claim IDs, any of the five anti-patterns, or an out-of-band ratio.

## G0 — narrative-outline review (the cheapest save)
Runs on the one-page `narrative_outline.md` (deck thesis + the meeting's intelligence questions + the chapter breakdown + each slide's one-sentence thesis and intended layout carrying Claim IDs, not prose). Because the deck is composed from the story, G0 judges both **storyline quality** and whether the **chapter breakdown and slide allocation fit the story** (the dominant events told as dedicated prose-led scenes, nothing over- or under-weighted), and returns **GREEN** (proceed), **AMBER** (proceed, named slides or the allocation need strengthening), or **RED** (story thin, wrong, or mis-shaped; fix before writing the plan). The orchestrator applies AMBER/RED fixes to the outline before building the content plan.

## G4 — content-substance review (the centrepiece)
An independent senior-strategist agent reads the whole content plan **before build** and judges, above all, whether the brief **explains the company in prose so a reader would understand it** (the story is told, not tabulated; the major events are explained in dedicated slides) and whether the **structure fits the story**. It also judges **depth**: it reads `coverage_log.md` against the `coverage` report and confirms the validated claims were each used, worked in, or consciously excluded with a sound reason, never silently dropped, and that the deck does not pad or repeat a layout lazily. It then scores correctness of the read, insight density, "so what" for I&L, narrative flow and honesty about gaps, returning per-slide, located, severity-tagged findings, each with a concrete instruction and a routing tag (`fix-in-plan` / `route-to-readiness` / `accept-with-note`). Any **HIGH** (a slide that says nothing, a wrong read, a missing must-have, a story reduced to fragments, or relevant validated material dropped without reason) blocks the freeze. Where `gate_runner.py spine` checks each answer-contract item is *present*, G4 judges it is *said well*: the executive read genuinely leads with the thesis and the one opportunity, the big-movers read names the two or three vectors and each one's demand direction, the supply-chain signature is company-specific rather than generic, and the opportunity intersection ties every angle to a sourced fact with incumbents acknowledged. It does not re-do G1 sourcing; it judges whether the brief is good, readable, sufficiently deep intelligence.

## Auditable, blocking, and fix-and-re-run with independence preserved
Each gate appends rows to its scorecard: `check | status (pass / fix-applied / strike / deviation) | claim or slide | one-line justification`, and ends in exactly one machine-read line, `STATUS: ALL-PASS` or `STATUS: BLOCKED`. `final_gate.py` keys only on that line. Three rules keep independence on re-runs:
1. **Every judgement re-run is a NEW isolated sub-agent** with fresh context, never the agent that raised the defect "checking its own fix".
2. **Reviewers never edit** the artefact; they return verdicts and defect rows, the orchestrator applies the fix.
3. **Bounded loops (cap ~3) with honest escalation**: if still failing, strike to the Meeting Brief or surface to the user in writing; never loosen a pass criterion to get through.

## Model/effort
Spend the most where a wrong answer compounds: G0, G2, G4, G7, G8 are Opus at High effort; G1b Opus medium; G6b Sonnet. Never step G2, G4, G7 or G8 down. The mechanical scripts gate first so the judgement agents never run against a plan that fails the free checks.

## Language (multilingual runs)
When `deck_meta.language` is not English, the deck and Meeting Brief are authored in that language (Latin-script European set only; see SKILL.md). Dispatch the **content/editorial reviewers in that language**: G0 (outline), G2 (evidence and honesty), G4 (content substance) and G8 (red-team) must read and judge the target-language prose; G6b (editorial) must judge the target-language register, applying the portable CBRE voice principles (`reference/content-and-tone.md`), not English idiom. Tell each: "the prose is in <language>; judge it in <language>." The mechanical gates are language-agnostic by construction (`personas` matches the persona answer-id tags, `reconcile`/`qa4` match shapes and dashes, not English words). **G1b (source verification) and G7 (visual render) need no language change** - G1b checks that sourced figures/quotes appear at source in their own language; G7 reads pixels. For any client-facing non-English deck, a **native-speaker review** is the final register check before delivery (the honest backstop, since Claude approximates rather than owns the brand voice in another language).

## Mechanical pre-checks (run first, in-thread)
Before any judgement reviewer is dispatched in Stage 4, run the cheapest deterministic checks so a malformed plan never reaches a build or a reviewer:
- `gate_runner.py validate-plan content_plan.json templates/content_plan.schema.json` - schema validity (uses `jsonschema` if installed, else a structural fallback). A plan that fails this is fixed before anything else.
- `gate_runner.py self-check templates/content_plan.schema.json helpers/build_deck.py` - asserts the schema's slide-kind and cell-kind enums equal the renderer's `KIND` and `CELL` dicts, so the docs and code cannot silently drift.
- `build_deck.py --dry-run` - resolves every slide and reports per-slide content without rendering; the real build is gated on a clean dry-run.
- `gate_runner.py envelope content_plan.json` - the **plan-time fit backstop**: using the renderer's own calibrated estimators and slot geometry, it surfaces fit risk before any render, which matters most on the **no-COM build path** (Linux / Cowork), where the two-pass PowerPoint bake cannot re-justify layout. It **hard-blocks only on unambiguous failures** (a headline/lead that consumes the whole body, or a cover title too long to fit even at the minimum size). Scene content that merely exceeds the body region is printed as a **non-blocking advisory**: that is the deck's deliberately dense design, which the renderer packs tight and which the **COM bake / G7 visual gate** adjudicate (a hard block there would be a false positive, since `measure_text` is deliberately conservative and a sum of per-row over-estimates over-counts the dense design). Treat the advisories as a checklist for G7; fix any real overflow by shortening copy, dropping a list/panel item, or splitting the slide.
- `gate_runner.py personas content_plan.json` - asserts the **three mandatory persona slides** ("what is on the mind of the CEO / CSCO / Head of Real Estate") are present; they are always in the deck (see `deck-structure.md`). A plan missing any of the three is fixed before the judgement reviewers run, and the Final Gate re-asserts it.
- `gate_runner.py spine content_plan.json` - asserts the **answer contract**: every REQUIRED answer (the executive read, company profile, strategy, financial story, big movers, supply-chain signature, operating model, network, challenge-to-real-estate, property set-up, recent movements, stakeholders, opportunity intersection, what-to-probe) is carried by a substantive slide or cell, tagged via `answers` (see `deck-structure.md`). A content floor, not a template: it never dictates slide count, order or layout, and G0 and G4 judge whether each answer is actually *said well*. Missing EXPECTED answers (`why-now`, `competitive-position`, `company-challenges`) print as an advisory, not a block.
- `gate_runner.py coverage intermediate/content_plan.json intermediate/source_ledger.csv` - the **depth-pass aid**: lists every validated (verified) claim the plan does not yet cite, grouped by agent, so the author deliberately works each into a slide, adds a slide, or records the exclusion (duplication or low relevance) in `coverage_log.md` before freezing. It is a review report that never blocks; G4 judges whether the coverage decisions are sound. Sourced material is never dropped silently.

The **canonical ledger location is `intermediate/source_ledger.csv`** (where `ledger.py merge` writes and every gate, plus `final_gate.py`, reads it); no copy to the briefing root is needed. The ledger `slide` column is derived from the plan, not entered by hand: `qa1` back-fills it before checking (default on), or run `ledger.py backfill-slides intermediate/content_plan.json intermediate/source_ledger.csv` explicitly and re-run after any plan edit. Struck (`verified=no`) and uncited rows stay blank.

## Reviewer verdict files (how independence is made auditable, not just asserted)
Each independent reviewer MUST write its verdict to `intermediate/reviews/<gate>.md` (G0, G1b, G2, G4 for the pre-build gate; G6b, G7, G8 for the post-build gate; G3 only if the forecast module is on), ending in one explicit machine-read line `VERDICT: <outcome>` (mirroring the scorecard `STATUS:` contract). The orchestrator pastes those verdicts into the scorecards. `gate_runner.py reviews <dir> {1|2}` and `final_gate.py` then assert every required verdict file exists, is non-empty with a recognisable verdict token, AND that its `VERDICT:` line is not a blocking outcome (`red` / `blocked` / `fix-required` / `reject`; a reviewer still holding an open HIGH records its line as `BLOCKED` or `fix-required`, never the bare phrase `open HIGH`, because `no open HIGH` is itself a clear and the check would otherwise invert it). This means the `STATUS: ALL-PASS` line cannot be earned without the reviewers having actually run, recorded a verdict, and that verdict being a clear: skipping a reviewer leaves a missing file and fails the gate, and a reviewer that blocked keeps the gate blocked until a fresh reviewer (after the fix) overwrites the file with a clear line. The orchestrator may not self-certify a pass over a reviewer that blocked. A reviewer's file is the artefact that proves separation of author and reviewer; without it, independence is unverifiable and the gate blocks.
